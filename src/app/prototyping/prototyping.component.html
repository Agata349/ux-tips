Prototyping

A prototype is a primitive representation or version of a product that a design team or front-end-development team typically creates during the design process. The goal of a prototype is to test the flow of a design solution and gather feedback on it — from both internal and external parties — before constructing the final product. The state of a prototype is fluid as the team revises the design iteratively based on user feedback.

  • Low Fidelity - e.g. a paper prototype or a basic wireframe. It's a good first step before moving on to an interactive prototype.

    • easy, quick and cheap; get your ideas down

    lack of details, not great for testing

  • High Fidelity - they are much more detailed, and their function is closer to that of the final product. High-fidelity prototypes are better for usability testing.

    • more realistic, easier for clients to grasp what the end product will look like

Tips

  • Don't get attached - if it's not working, get rid of it
  • Always use the largest practical sample size
  • Be consistent!
  • The bigger testing group, the better
  • Avoid leading questions
  • No guarantees - remember, the point of testing is to mitigate risk, not remove risk
  • Don't spend too much time on it
