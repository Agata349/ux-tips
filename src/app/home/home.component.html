What is the difference between UI & UX?

UX (User Experience) in a few words: functionality of the product (web application, furniture or a bottle of shampoo!). UX includes all aspects of the end user's interaction with the company, its services, and its products. In other words connects with each other technology, design and business. UI (User Interface) is everything what the user sees: colors, typography, distance between elements and much more!

What is design thinking?

Design thinking is a process that seeks and to solve complex problems by taking into account what the users' needs are.

  1. Empathize - Research Your Users' Needs
  2. Define - State Your Users' Needs and Problems
  3. Ideate - Challenge Assumptions and Create Ideas
  4. Prototype - Start to Create Solutions
  5. Test - Try Your Solutions Out

The most common roles in UX

  • UX Designer - creates prototypes, wireframes
  • UX/UI Designer - creates prototypes, takes care of visual design
  • UX Researcher - conducts user interviews and usability testing, designs survey
  • Information Architect - creates wireframes, navigation and hierarchy, analyses user research
  • Front-End Developer - writes functional code, uses HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • UX Copywriter - takes care of the language which is the most convenient for the users
  • Visual/Graphic Designer - creates icons and visual elements for UI, create and applies styles

Types of research

  1. Qualitative
    • In-depth interviews - asking open-ended questions verbally to respondents
    • Focus groups - discussion among a group of people about a topic to gather opinions that can be used for further research.
    • Ethnography - Participating in a community or organization for an extended period of time to closely observe culture and behavior
  2. Quantitative
    • Surveys - list of closed or multiple choice questions that is distributed to a sample (online, in person, or over the phone)

When to use qualitative vs. quantitative research

  • Quantitative research - helps to confirm or test a theory or hypothesis
  • Qualitative research - helps to understand concepts, thoughts, experiences
